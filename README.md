# Phishing Awareness Training

A comprehensive phishing awareness training module built as part of the CodeAlpha Cybersecurity Internship. Includes an interactive HTML web module with a quiz and a professional PowerPoint presentation covering all major phishing attack types, red flags, real-world case studies, and defense strategies.

## Contents

- `index.html` : Interactive single-page awareness module with 6 sections and a 5-question quiz
- `PhishingAwareness.pptx` : 8-slide professional presentation covering the same content
- `README.md` : This file

## Features

- **6 Learning Sections**: Overview, attack types, red flags, real-world case studies, defense tips, and a quiz
- **Interactive Quiz**: 5 questions with instant feedback, score tracking, and a retry option
- **Real-World Examples**: PayPal phishing scam, Google & Facebook $100M BEC attack, Pakistan bank SMS scam
- **10 Red Flags**: A checklist of warning signs to spot phishing emails and fake websites
- **Defense Strategies**: Practical steps including 2FA, link verification, password managers, and reporting

## Topics Covered

### Types of Phishing Attacks

- **Email Phishing**: Mass emails impersonating banks, services, or companies
- **Spear Phishing**: Targeted attacks using personalized information
- **Smishing**: Phishing via SMS text messages
- **Vishing**: Voice phishing via phone calls
- **Whaling**: Targeted attacks on executives and high-value individuals
- **Clone Phishing**: Legitimate emails cloned and resent with malicious links

### Key Red Flags

- Suspicious or mismatched sender domain
- Urgent or threatening language
- Generic greetings such as "Dear Customer"
- Links that don't match the displayed text
- Unexpected attachments (.exe, .zip, .docm)
- Poor grammar and spelling
- Requests for passwords or sensitive data
- Offers that seem too good to be true
- Mismatched branding or logos
- Missing HTTPS on login pages

### Defense Best Practices

- Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all accounts
- Hover over links to verify URLs before clicking
- Call back using official numbers to verify requests
- Use a password manager to avoid filling credentials on fake sites
- Report suspicious emails to your IT or security team
- Keep software and browsers up to date

## Real-World Case Studies

### Case 1: Fake PayPal Email

Millions of users received emails claiming their PayPal account was limited. A fake login page harvested credentials. The sender domain was `paypal-secure-alerts.com` instead of `paypal.com`.

**Lesson**: Always verify the sender domain. Never click links in unsolicited emails.

### Case 2: Google & Facebook : $100M Loss

Between 2013 and 2015, attacker Evaldas Rimasauskas sent fake invoices to Google and Facebook impersonating a legitimate Taiwanese hardware vendor. Both companies wired over $100 million before the fraud was detected.

**Lesson**: Always verify wire transfer requests through a second channel such as a direct phone call.

### Case 3: Pakistan Bank SMS Scam

Customers received SMS messages claiming their accounts were blocked. When they called the provided number, attackers posing as bank agents collected OTP codes and drained accounts.

**Lesson**: Banks never ask for OTPs via SMS or phone. Always call back using the number on the back of your card.
